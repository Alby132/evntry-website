The problem

Promoting your gigs
shouldn't be your
second job

Every musician deals with the same three headaches.

Pain 01

Your fans don't know you have a gig

You post on Instagram. Half your followers don't see it. The venue posts maybe, maybe not. Your loyal fans miss the show because the algorithm didn't surface it.

Pain 02

You have no home base

Spotify is for streams. Instagram is for clips. Bandcamp is for merch. Nowhere lists "every gig this person is playing" — so fans never know where to look.

Pain 03

Venues find you the hard way

Bookers stumble onto you through other bands, word of mouth, late-night Instagram scrolls. No easy way for a venue to find a sax player or a punk trio when they need one.
